<p>
A careful skincare routine forms the foundation of psoriasis management. While specific medications treat inflammation, daily skincare minimizes irritation, reduces symptoms, and supports healing. Gentle cleansing, appropriate moisturizing, and mindful practices prevent flare-ups and improve quality of life.
</p>

<h2>Gentle Cleansing</h2>
<p>
Avoid harsh soaps and cleansers that disrupt skin barrier and worsen psoriasis. Use fragrance-free, gentle cleansers specifically formulated for sensitive skin. Lukewarm water is better than hot water—heat increases itching and irritation. Bathe rather than shower when possible to allow skin soaking. Pat skin dry gently rather than rubbing. Avoid abrasive washcloths or loofahs that can injure psoriatic skin.
</p>

<h2>Moisturizing Strategy</h2>
<p>
Moisturizing is critical—psoriatic skin is inherently dry. Apply moisturizers immediately after bathing while skin is damp to maximize hydration. Ointments (thicker, greasy formulations) penetrate better than lotions. Fragrance-free options prevent irritation. Thicker creams suit dry areas while lighter lotions suit face. Reapply throughout day as needed, especially to affected areas. Heavy nightly applications support overnight healing.
</p>

<h2>Medication Application</h2>
<p>
Apply topical medications to damp skin after cleansing for better absorption. Use two minutes between different medications for optimal penetration. Don't mix medications unless specifically instructed. Allow medications to dry before dressing to prevent staining. Occlusion (wrapping) enhance absorption for thick plaques. Rotate between affected areas to prevent tolerance.
</p>

    alt="Psoriasis skincare tips"
    style="max-width:100%; border-radius:8px;"
  />
</div>

<h2>Avoiding Triggers</h2>
<p>
Identify personal triggers through careful observation. Common irritants include fragrances, dyes, sulfites, and preservatives. Wool and synthetic fabrics often irritate more than cotton. Perfumed products (shampoos, soaps, lotions) frequently worsen symptoms. Label reading helps identify and avoid problematic products. Hypoallergenic, fragrance-free products are usually safer choices.
</p>

<h2>Special Considerations for Body Areas</h2>
<p>
Scalp psoriasis requires medicated shampoos combined with topical treatments. Gentle brushing and picking avoidance prevents worsening. Facial psoriasis needs particularly gentle care—use lowest-potency corticosteroids and non-irritating cleansers. Genital involvement requires extra gentleness and appropriate medical treatment. Nail care includes keeping nails short and avoiding harsh manicure techniques.
</p>

<h2>Bathing Recommendations</h2>
<p>
Lukewarm baths with limited time (10-15 minutes) prevent excessive skin drying. Oatmeal baths (colloidal oatmeal) soothe irritated skin. Bath oils may help but can make tub slippery. Adding table salt to baths may help though evidence is mixed. Avoid baths after strenuous exercise when skin is already irritated.
</p>

<p>
A consistent, gentle skincare routine prevents many psoriasis flare-ups and supports medical treatment effectiveness.
