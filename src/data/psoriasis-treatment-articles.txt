<h2>Topical Corticosteroids</h2>
<p>
Topical corticosteroids remain first-line therapy for most psoriasis patients. They reduce inflammation and slow plaques effectively. Potency ranges from low to very high, matching disease severity and anatomical location. Low-potency steroids suit sensitive areas like face and skin folds. High-potency steroids treat thick plaques on palms and soles. Duration of continuous use should be limited—extended use causes skin atrophy. Steroid sparing agents combined with steroids reduce long-term complications.
</p>

<h2>Vitamin D Analogs</h2>
<p>
Calcipotriol and calcitriol (vitamin D3 analogs) slow skin cell proliferation and reduce inflammation. They work well combined with topical corticosteroids. Vitamin D analogs can be used longer-term. They may cause irritation or vitamin D absorption if applied too extensively. Fingernail staining occasionally occurs. Effectiveness is comparable to mid-potency corticosteroids when used appropriately.
</p>

<h2>Topical Retinoids</h2>
<p>
Tazarotene, a topical retinoid, reduces inflammation and skin cell turnover. It suits patients unable to tolerate corticosteroids. Retinoids commonly cause initial irritation—start low concentration and increase gradually. Avoid sun exposure during use. Effectiveness rivals mid-potency corticosteroids. Retinoids may be particularly helpful for scalp and folded area psoriasis.
</p>

<h2>Coal Tar and Salicylic Acid</h2>
<p>
Coal tar has decades of safe use, though less popular nowadays. It reduces inflammation and scaling, though smells unpleasant and stains clothing. Salicylic acid shampoos help remove scales and improve topical medication penetration. These agents often work best combined with other topicals.
</p>

<h2>Combination Strategies</h2>
<p>
Alternating different topical agents reduces steroid tolerance (tachyphylaxis). Combining corticosteroid with vitamin D analog often works better than either alone. Using lower-potency steroids combined with steroid-sparing agents reduces side effects. Rotation between different potencies or drug classes optimizes long-term control.
</p>
